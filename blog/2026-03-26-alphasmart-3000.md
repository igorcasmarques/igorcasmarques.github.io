I have three portable, distraction-free devices. Two are thermal typewriters: a [Brother EP-20](https://typewriterdatabase.com/1983-brother-ep20.5137.typewriter) and a [Casiowriter CW-11](https://typewriterdatabase.com/Casio.Casiowriter+CW-10.937.bmys). I love them both, but neither are my favourite. That title goes to a dinky little word processor from the turn of the millennium: the [AlphaSmart 3000](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaSmart).

The AlphaSmart 3000 is basically a small keyboard with an LCD screen on top. It runs on three AA batteries that last a *very* long time. I've used mine for hours and hours and hours, and the battery icon still shows 3/4 bars.

The internal memory is capable of storing eight files, each file storing something like 12.5 pages, according to Wikipedia. I usually use the AlphaSmart for drafting scenes before transferring them to my PC, so file size has never been an issue for me.

You type just like you would at a computer. As you type, the text appears on the LCD screen, which displays four lines at a time, 40 characters per line. Unlike a typewriter, you can type and re-type and fix mistakes freely on the AlphaSmart, but the small display and slow navigation also prevents you from editing too much as you write. Best of both worlds, if you ask me.

The screen isn't backlit. This means that if I type in bed at night, I have to use my reading light. The upside is that direct sunlight doesn't affect battery life like it does to our mobile devices. On the contrary, bright light makes the text on the LCD screen even clearer. So it's great for typing while laying in my hammock outside.

When I'm done drafting, it's time to transfer the text to the computer. At first, I was really worried about this part. I thought there'd be issues with hardware compatibility since the AlphaSmart is ancient tech in computer terms. Or that it would export a file in an weird and unsupported extension.

But the AlphaSmart is, well, smart. And future-proof. It uses a USB-B cable to connect to the computer. These cables are still abundant because printers use them. I have three of them. When you plug the AlphaSmart in, the computer recognizes it as a plain keyboard. Then, you select the file that has your draft, hit the **send** key, and the AlphaSmart retypes the text on whatever program you have open.

It's super simple and super elegant.
